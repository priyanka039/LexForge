Under Indian contract law, anticipatory breach occurs when a party to a contract indicates, before the time for performance arrives, that they will not or cannot perform their contractual obligations. § Hadley v Baxendale

The Supreme Court in Saradamani Kandappan v. S. Rajalakshmi CASE-041 held that such repudiation entitles the aggrieved party to either accept the repudiation and sue for damages immediately, or affirm the contract and wait for actual breach.

The doctrine of anticipatory repudiation was further clarified in Frost v. Knight CASE-089 where the court established the "election" principle — the innocent party's right to choose their remedy.

Issue 1 of 3 · Employment Law
Wrongful Termination of Fixed-Term Contract
◯ Strong Argument
I
Issue
What is the central legal question?
Whether the defendant company's termination of the plaintiff's fixed-term employment contract prior to its expiry, without cause, notice, or severance payment, constitutes wrongful termination and breach of contract entitling the plaintiff to damages under Section 73 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872.
R
Rule
Applicable law and precedents
Under Section 73 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872, a party who suffers by reason of breach of a contract is entitled to receive compensation for any loss or damage caused to him thereby. § ICA §73

The Supreme Court in DTC v. DTC Mazdoor Congress CASE-112 held that fixed-term employees cannot be terminated before the term expires without lawful cause or contractual provision allowing such termination.

Further, in State of Punjab v. Anoop Kumar Tiwari CASE-078, it was established that corporate restructuring does not automatically constitute a lawful ground for early termination of a fixed-term contract unless expressly provided therein.
A
Application
Applying law to the facts
Applying the above rules to the present case: the plaintiff's contract was for a fixed term of 2 years commencing 1 January 2023, with no express termination clause permitting early exit due to restructuring. The defendant's termination on 15 August 2023 — a mere 7.5 months into the term — was without cause, without notice, and without severance.

The defendant's reliance on "restructuring" does not satisfy the threshold established in Anoop Kumar Tiwari CASE-078, as that ground was not a term of the contract. The failure to provide notice and severance further compounds the breach and entitles the plaintiff to expectation damages for the remaining 16.5 months of the contract.
C
Conclusion
Summary and remedy
The plaintiff is entitled to succeed on this issue. The defendant's early termination constitutes a wrongful breach of a fixed-term employment contract. The plaintiff is entitled to claim expectation damages equivalent to the remaining salary and benefits for 16.5 months, plus any consequential losses proved at trial, subject to the duty to mitigate.

Counter — Mitigation of Loss
Defendant's Primary Rebuttal
The plaintiff has a legal duty to mitigate their losses following termination. § ICA §73 expressly provides that compensation is not awarded for loss that the claimant could have avoided by reasonable steps. The defendant will adduce evidence that the plaintiff failed to actively seek alternate employment for an unreasonable period, thereby inflating their claimed damages.

Further, the existence of an implied restructuring authorization CASE-203 within the employment terms may justify the early termination under the industrial relations framework.
